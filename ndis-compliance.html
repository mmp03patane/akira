Built for the NDIS — What Your Website Actually Needs

Most web designers will hand you a pretty website and disappear. We build websites that satisfy the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission, protect your registration, and build trust with participants and families from day one.

We Know What Auditors Look For

When the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission reviews your practice, your website is one of the first things they examine. It needs to reflect your professionalism, your compliance, and your commitment to participant safety.

We've studied the NDIS Practice Standards, the Audit Framework, and the requirements of the Quality and Safeguards Commission — and we build every website with these in mind. Here's exactly what we address.

01

Legal & Business Identification

Your website must clearly display your registered business name exactly as registered, your ABN, full contact details, and your NDIS registration status. We build this into every site as standard — no exceptions.

  • Registered business name as per ASIC
  • ABN displayed in footer and contact page
  • Phone, email, and address clearly accessible
  • NDIS registration status and registration number
  • Registration groups you are approved to deliver
✓ Included in every Akira build
02

Required Policies — Accessible & Visible

Auditors check whether participants can access your policies easily. These cannot be buried or missing. We build a dedicated Policies page and link it prominently from your footer and navigation.

  • Privacy Policy (mandatory under Australian Privacy Act)
  • Complaints & Feedback Policy
  • Incident Management Policy
  • Code of Conduct
  • Cancellation Policy
  • Service Agreement terms
  • Child Safety Policy (if working with minors)
  • Restrictive Practices Policy (if applicable)
✓ Policy page template provided with every build
03

Complaints & Feedback Pathway

This is one of the most common audit failures. Your website must clearly outline how participants can lodge a complaint, your response timeframe, and their right to escalate to the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission.

  • Dedicated complaints form or contact method
  • Expected response timeframe stated
  • Reference to NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission escalation
  • Transparent and easy to find — not buried in a PDF
✓ Built into your site structure as standard
04

Accessibility — WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

NDIS providers serve people with disability. Your website must reflect that commitment through genuine accessibility — not as an afterthought, but as a foundation. Auditors notice when providers claim to champion inclusion but operate an inaccessible website.

  • WCAG 2.1 AA compliant design and structure
  • Alt text on all images
  • High contrast text ratios
  • Clear and consistent font sizing
  • Keyboard navigation compatibility
  • Simple, plain-language copy
  • Read-aloud plugin available on request
  • Easy English option available on request
✓ WCAG 2.1 AA built into every Akira site
05

Clear Service Descriptions

Vague service descriptions raise red flags. Your website must clearly outline what you provide, who you serve, and under what funding arrangements — with no misleading claims.

  • Clear description of every service offered
  • Eligibility criteria stated
  • Geographic coverage clearly listed
  • Funding types accepted: self-managed, plan-managed, NDIA-managed
  • No exaggerated or misleading service claims
✓ Service page structure guided by our discovery process
06

Transparent Pricing

If you advertise pricing, it must align with the NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits. Vague pricing language can raise compliance concerns and erode participant trust.

  • Pricing aligned to current NDIS Price Guide
  • No hidden fees or misleading cost structures
  • Clear cancellation and refund terms
  • Link to NDIS Pricing Arrangements where appropriate
✓ Pricing structure reviewed during onboarding
07

Participant Rights Statement

Every NDIS provider website should include a clear statement of participant rights. This directly aligns with the NDIS Practice Standards and demonstrates your commitment to person-centred practice.

  • Right to dignity and respect
  • Freedom from abuse, neglect, and exploitation
  • Right to informed consent
  • Right to withdraw from services
  • Right to make complaints without fear
✓ Participant Rights page template included
08

Data Security & Form Handling

Any form collecting participant information must meet Australian privacy standards. We build secure, encrypted data collection into every site — this is non-negotiable.

  • SSL certificate — all sites run on HTTPS
  • Encrypted form submissions
  • Clear consent checkbox on all intake forms
  • Transparent explanation of data storage and use
  • No unnecessary sensitive data collection
  • Privacy Act compliant data handling
✓ Secure by default on every Akira build
09

Worker Screening Transparency

If you list staff or workers, your website should state that all workers hold current NDIS Worker Screening Checks and relevant qualifications. You don't need to publish certificates — a clear statement of compliance is sufficient.

  • Worker screening compliance statement
  • NDIS Worker Screening Check noted
  • Working With Children Check noted where applicable
  • Qualifications and experience noted per worker
✓ Staff compliance statement included in our templates
10

No Misleading Marketing

Under Australian Consumer Law and NDIS guidelines, your website cannot make guaranteed funding claims, imply NDIS endorsement, or publish false testimonials. We review all copy before launch.

  • No "guaranteed NDIS funding" claims
  • No implication of NDIS Commission endorsement
  • All testimonials genuine and verifiable
  • Accurate representation of services and outcomes
✓ Copy reviewed for compliance before every launch
